About Dowthwaite Head Farm

Dowthwaite Head Farm is a historic farm located in the beautiful Matterdale Valley in the Lake District, Cumbria. This farm, with a history spanning over five centuries, is a living testament to the agricultural traditions of the region. Today, it remains a symbol of sustainable farming practices and community engagement.

The History of Dowthwaite Head Farm

The history of Dowthwaite Head Farm dates back to the 1500s, when the Grisdale family first began farming this land. Over the years, the farm has seen various changes, yet it has always remained a central part of the local community. Today, it stands as a reminder of the rich agricultural legacy of the Lake District.

Our Commitment to Sustainability

At Dowthwaite Head Farm, we are committed to using sustainable farming methods that respect the land, promote biodiversity, and contribute to the local economy. We focus on ethical farming practices such as rotational grazing, minimal pesticide use, and land conservation to ensure the long-term health of our farm and the environment.

Looking to the Future

Looking ahead, Dowthwaite Head Farm aims to become a hub for sustainable agriculture and education. We plan to engage the local community by offering educational programs about regenerative farming practices, sustainable land management, and the importance of preserving traditional farming techniques.
